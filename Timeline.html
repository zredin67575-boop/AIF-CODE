TIME LINE OF OUR UNDERSTANDING OF SPACE

Humanity's understanding of space has grown from looking at constellations and planets through the human eye to sending rovers and collecting samples. Take a look at where it began and where it's heading:

BEFORE 500 BCE

ANCIENT ASTRONOMY

  • Early civilizations observed the sky to track time and seasons
  • Constellations were used for navigation, storytelling, and calendars
  • Cultures like Indigenous Australians, Egyptians, and Mesopotamians all had unique sky knowledge
    • Ancient astronomy is where it all started, by simply looking up.

    Ancient cultures mapped the sky long before telescopes.

    500 BCE - 1500 CE

    CLASSICAL ASTRONOMY

  • Aritotle suggested Earth was the centre of the universe.
  • Ptolemy developed the geocentric (Earth-centric) model.
  • Astronomy was based on observation, but limited by belief systems.

    • The geocentric model placed the Earth at the centre of the universe.

    16th - 17th CENT.

    THE SCIENTIFIC REVOLUTION

  • Galileo Galilei used a telescope to observe moons, planets, and stars.
  • Nicolaus Copernicus proposed the Sun-centred (heliocentric) model.
  • Johnanes Kepler discovered planets move in elliptical orbits.

    • The heliocentric model overturned the long-held geocentric model, with further confirmation from Galileo's observations and Kepler's elliptical orbits.

    17th - 18th CENT.

    LAWS OF PHYSICS

  • Isaac Newton explained gravity and motion, his laws explained how planets move and why.
  • Newton's first law, the law of inertia (an object's ability to resist motion) states that an object at rest will stay at rest and an object in motion will stay in motion unless acted upon by an unbalanced force.
  • His second law, the acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the net force acting on it and inversely proportional to its mass, shifted the view of space from a mystical realm into a predicatable and measurable one.
  • Newton's third law states that every action will have an equal and opposite reaction. If object A exerts a force on object B, object B simultaneously exerts a force of equal magnitude and opposite direction on object A
  • His 'fourth' law, or the law of universal gravitation, which states every object with mass is attracted to other objects with mass with a force directly proportional to the product of their masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them.

    • A diagram from Isaac Newton's Philosophiae Naturalis Principia Mathematica.

    20th CENT.

    EXPANDING UNIVERSE

  • Albert Einstein developed relativity (space and time are connected).
  • Edwin Hubble discovered galaxies beyond ours.
  • Scientists realised the universe is expanding.

    • His theory specify how the local density of matter and energy determines the geometry of space-time.

    20th CENT - PRES.

    SPACE AGE

  • First satellites launche - the Soviet Union launched Sputnik 1 on October 4th, 1957. Sputnik 2 closely followed, carryig Laika the dog.
  • Humans travelled to space.
  • Neil Armstrong walks on the moon (1969)
  • Space telescopes like Hubble and James Webb explore deep space.
    • We went from only looking at space, to exploring it.

    Sputnik 1 was the first artificial satellite, and initiated the USSR-US space race.

    TODAY

    MODERN ASTRONOMY

  • Study of black holes, dark matter, and exoplanets.
  • Powerful telescopes like the James Webb Space Telescope see the early universe
  • Ongoing search for life beyond Earth

    • Messier 87 was the first black hole ever imaged, in 2019 by the Event Horizon Telescope.

    FUTURE

    WHO KNOWS?

  • Missions to Mars and Beyond.
  • Search for extraterrestrial life.
  • New physics and astrophysics discoveries.

    • A Mars base is a proposed, sustainable human habitat deisgned for long-term exploration.
